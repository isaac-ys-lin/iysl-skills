# Decision

## Source Type

Workflow migration brief comparing a previous manual mode with a newer verified agent-assisted mode.

## Primary Claim

The important story is the contrast between two operating modes: ad hoc manual work versus verified, agent-assisted work.

## Relation

Contrast and transformation between two comparable states.

## Layout

`before_after`

## Why This Fits

The reader needs to compare the same job under two modes and immediately see what changed in reliability, evidence, and control. A before/after split keeps the comparison tight and aligns with the review question better than expanding every internal step.

## Rejected Alternatives

A `stack` would show components but hide the transition. A `timeline` would imply a rollout schedule instead of an operating contrast. A `flow` would overemphasize branching mechanics and dilute the headline change in working style.

## Style And Animation

Use restrained visual contrast: the "before" side can feel noisier or less structured, while the "after" side should feel clearer and more deliberate. Animation should help the eye compare left and right, not distract from the stable state comparison.

## Validation

Confirm the brief clearly supports a two-state contrast. The spec must pass `--verify --check`, and the static PNG should make the transformation legible even without motion.
