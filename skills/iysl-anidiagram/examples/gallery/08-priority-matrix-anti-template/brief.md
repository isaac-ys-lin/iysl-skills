# Prioritization Brief

A product lead needs to compare proposed initiatives by likely impact and expected effort during roadmap planning. The source includes items that are urgent-sounding, but there is no real chronology between them.

The review needs to answer:

- Which bets are high-impact enough to prioritize now?
- Which items are expensive enough to defer, split, or challenge?
- Which easy wins can move quickly without pretending they happen first in time?

This case should explicitly reject the temptation to turn prioritization into a fake timeline.
