# Product Launch Source Brief

A product team is preparing a mid-sized feature launch for a workflow tool used by operations managers and analysts. The team has already aligned on the feature promise, but launch readiness is still uneven across rollout planning, onboarding, support coverage, and post-launch learning.

Key facts:

- The launch needs named gates so legal review, support prep, and release controls happen in order.
- Adoption will narrow at each handoff from reached audience to active retained users.
- Readiness is not only a sequence problem. It also depends on how launch planning, user activation, and issue-learning connect on one page.
- The audience is cross-functional: product, operations, support, and leadership.

The diagram should help a reviewer decide what the story is: gated execution, adoption narrowing, or one-page launch readiness.
