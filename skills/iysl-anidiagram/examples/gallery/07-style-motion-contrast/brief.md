# Support Handoff Brief

A service operations team tracks the same four-stage support path: intake, triage, specialist work, and customer closeout. Two stakeholders need different readings from the same semantic process.

- One wants to show where active attention is right now as focus moves along the path.
- Another wants to show how ownership is explicitly handed forward from one role to the next.

The underlying order is identical, but the motion story changes the meaning. This case exists to show that style and animation choices can change emphasis without changing the semantic layout.
