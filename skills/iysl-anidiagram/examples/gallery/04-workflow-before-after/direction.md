# Direction

## Claim

Same recurring task, two operating modes: ad hoc manual work runs on memory and luck, while the verified agent-assisted mode produces evidence at every checkpoint.

## Relation

Contrast / before-after. Two comparable states of the same job; the story is what changed between them, not a rollout sequence.

## Audience

Team members and leads deciding whether to adopt the verified workflow: they need to compare the two modes checkpoint by checkpoint, not memorize sub-steps.

## Fact List

| F9 | The diagram should highlight the operating-mode shift, not every sub-step | droppable (design constraint, not diagram content) |

## Animation Story

Alternating emphasis between the two panels: the Before side holds the reader's focus for half the loop, then focus crosses to the After side, forever alternating with no snap. Per the Contrast / before-after row of `references/animation-semantics.md`: alternating emphasis between the two sides is the required motion; a single flowing dot on one side would misread the diagram as a process. The motion says "compare these two states" — nothing travels, nothing progresses.
